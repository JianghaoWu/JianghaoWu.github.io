In recent years, with the continuous increase in the number of vehicles in China, the construction of urban parking facilities has lagged far behind. As a result, the problem of parking difficulties has become increasingly severe. This issue not only significantly impacts people's quality of life but also adds to the challenges of managing urban public environments such as residential communities and roads.

To respond to the national call and address the parking challenges, this project introduces an innovative Z-shaped curb-free parking device as the technological core. Drawing inspiration from the business model of shared bicycles, the project focuses on the parking needs of vehicle owners in older residential communities. It introduces a "Shared Parking Spaces" service where vehicle owners can search for parking spaces, park their vehicles, and make payments through a mobile app. This service aims to alleviate the parking problem, offering convenience and efficiency while enhancing the overall parking experience.
